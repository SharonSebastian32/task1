Artificial intelligence (AI) refers to the development of computer systems capable of performing tasks that normally require human intelligence. These tasks include problem-solving, learning, reasoning, language understanding, and visual perception. AI encompasses various subfields, such as machine learning, natural language processing, computer vision, and robotics. With advancements in algorithms and computational power, AI has become integral to industries like healthcare, finance, and autonomous systems, offering solutions such as predictive analytics, personalized recommendations, and automation. AI can be categorized into narrow AI, which specializes in specific tasks, and general AI, which aims to replicate human cognitive abilities. While narrow AI is already widely implemented, general AI remains a theoretical goal. The rapid evolution of AI is transforming business processes, enhancing decision-making, and driving innovations across many sectors, though it also raises ethical concerns about privacy, job displacement, and decision transparency. As AI continues to evolve, its potential to shape the future of technology and society is immense.

Machine learning is a subset of artificial intelligence (AI) that focuses on developing algorithms and models that enable computers to learn from and make decisions based on data. It involves the use of statistical techniques to give computers the ability to identify patterns, make predictions, and improve performance without explicit programming. Machine learning models are trained on large datasets and can perform tasks like image recognition, natural language processing, and recommendation systems. Supervised, unsupervised, and reinforcement learning are the primary types of machine learning, each suited to different types of problems. Supervised learning uses labeled data to train models, while unsupervised learning works with unlabeled data to discover hidden patterns. Reinforcement learning, on the other hand, focuses on decision-making in environments by maximizing cumulative rewards. As machine learning technology evolves, it is becoming increasingly integral to industries such as healthcare, finance, and autonomous systems, enabling smarter, more efficient solutions. Its growing impact on automation, personalization, and predictive analytics is reshaping the future of technology.
